# Ferrari N.V. (RACE) BEV Valuation

## Overview

This project evaluates Ferrari N.V. (NYSE: RACE) through a 10-year Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation model. The analysis focuses on the long-term implications of Ferrari's transition toward battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and whether the company's luxury-brand economics can withstand increasing performance standardization across the automotive industry.

While Ferrari has historically operated more like a scarcity-driven luxury house than a traditional automaker, the transition to electrification introduces new risks to pricing power, margin durability, and competitive differentiation.

**Recommendation:** Short Ferrari (RACE)

---

## Investment Thesis

Ferrari's economic moat has historically relied on four key drivers:

* Controlled production volumes
* Strong pricing power
* Premium operating margins
* Exceptional returns on invested capital

The company's transition toward hybrid vehicles has not weakened these advantages. However, the shift to full battery electric vehicles creates a structural tension:

> Ferrari's value proposition depends on emotional differentiation, mechanical distinction, and exclusivity, while BEV technology increasingly standardizes performance characteristics such as acceleration and torque across manufacturers.

If ultra-performance vehicles become more technologically commoditized, Ferrari may be forced to rely more heavily on symbolic brand value rather than engineering differentiation to sustain premium pricing.

## Methodology

### Revenue Forecasting

* Segmented Ferrari revenue into automotive and non-automotive businesses
* Projected average selling prices (ASP) and shipment growth through 2035
* Modeled Ferrari's target mix of:

  * 40% Internal Combustion Engine (ICE)
  * 40% Hybrid
  * 20% BEV

### Operating Forecasts

* Forecasted EBIT margins using historical transition and stabilization periods
* Modeled elevated R&D spending during the BEV rollout
* Estimated reinvestment needs using invested capital, ROIC, and revenue growth assumptions

### Discounted Cash Flow Analysis

* Built a 10-year FCFF valuation model
* Calculated WACC using:

  * Beta: 1.07
  * Risk-Free Rate: 2.79%
  * Market Risk Premium: 5.50%
* Applied a 3.0% terminal growth rate

### Sensitivity Analysis

Stress-tested valuation outcomes across:

* EBIT margins
* WACC assumptions
* Terminal growth rates
* Beta estimates

---

## Key Findings

* Ferrari generated revenue growth despite largely flat shipment volumes, highlighting exceptional pricing power.
* Hybrid adoption has not materially weakened margins or returns on capital.
* Ferrari's long-term valuation remains highly sensitive to assumptions regarding margin durability during the BEV transition.
* The primary risk is not electrification itself, but the potential erosion of Ferrari's differentiation if performance becomes increasingly standardized across luxury manufacturers.
